Glaucus Atlanticus (Blue Sea Slug)

Glaucus atlanticus feeds on venmous creatures, including the Portuguese man o' war. They store the stinging nematocysts from these creatures in their tissue to protect them from predators.

  • Average Length: Up to 3 cm in length
  • Habitat: Warm temperate climates in the southern pacific
  • Average Lifespan: 1 year
  • Ability: Immune to venomous nematocysts

The Glaucus Atlanticus uses countershading. The blue side of their body faces upwards towards the sky, blending with the blue of the water. Underneath they have a silver or grey side that faces downwards, blending in with the sunlight when viewed upwards from underwater. These creatures are pelagic, meaning they float upside down by using the surface tension of the water. Humans handling the slug may receive a very painful and potentially dangerous sting.
